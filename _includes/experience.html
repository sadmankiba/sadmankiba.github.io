Predicting Sequence Features of DNA Bendability and Chromosome Conformation with Deep Learning

Mar, 2021 - May, 2022

The mechanical properties of genomic DNA, such as its bendability, play a strong role in its function and structure. We hypothesized that deep learning models trained on DNA bendability datasets could improve the models’ quality of fit and reveal the sequence signatures underlying DNA bendability. To pursue this hypothesis, we fitted interpretable convolutional neural networks on recent DNA bendability datasets. Using feature attribution analysis, we computed the sequence signatures (motifs) associated with increased or decreased DNA bendability.

Transparent Third-party Authentication and Application Mobility in Federated Edge Computing

Feb, 2020 - Oct, 2021

Federating edge providers expands capability, capacity, and coverage but gives rise to two issues—authentication and application mobility—for continuous service during roaming. We proposed a mechanism that uses a transparent proxy to transfer the information of both authentication and application state across edge providers to resolve these issues. We optimized this mechanism by prefetching information and reusing tokens stored in UE. We designed solution architecture and protocols for two scenarios: Inter-MEC federation and MEC-fog federation. Our methods reduced service interruption latency by 73.6%-84.6% compared to cloud-based handover.
