Feb, 2020 - Oct, 2021

Transparent Third-party Authentication and Application Mobility in Federated Edge Computing

Federating edge providers expands capability, capacity, and coverage but gives rise to two issues—authentication and application mobility—for continuous service during roaming. We propose a mechanism that uses a transparent proxy to transfer the information of both authentication and application state across edge providers to resolve these issues. We optimize this mechanism by prefetching information and reusing tokens stored in UE. We design solution architecture and protocols for two scenarios: Inter-MEC federation and MEC-fog federation. Our methods reduce service interruption latency by 73.6%-84.6% compared to cloud-based handover.

Jan, 2020 - Present

IoT-VR: IoT Simulation Platform for Cost-free Deployment and Monitoring of IoT Network

Current IoT simulation platforms provide a simple user interface to simulate IoT components, failing to provide a real-world-like environment. They also do not support recent technologies, such as edge computing. We address this gap by developing an IoT simulation platform with a virtual world where users can deploy and monitor IoT networks. So, we simulate attributes of both real-world environment and IoT devices. We have developed simulation of various IoT devices and sensors, such as Arduino, GPS and ultrasonic sensor. Our software is presently being used in the course UIUC CS 437 Internet of Things.

Predicting Sequence Features of DNA Bendability and Chromosome Conformation with Deep Learning

The mechanical properties of genomic DNA, such as its bendability, play a strong role in its function and structure. We hypothesized that deep learning models trained on DNA bendability datasets could improve the models’ quality of fit and reveal the sequence signatures underlying DNA bendability. To pursue this hypothesis, we fit interpretable convolutional neural networks on recent DNA bendability datasets. Using feature attribution analysis, we compute the sequence signatures (motifs) associated with increased or decreased DNA bendability.
